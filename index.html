While I’m exploring the wide range of roles in tech, I’m especially interested in applying my skills in software development—whether that’s web, mobile, or backend. I see myself as a flexible developer, ready to take on challenges, learn quickly, and grow into a specialized role through real-world experience.

About me — A curious and creative mind who stumbled into tech through a love for storytelling.



At 13, I started designing character sheets and layouts in HTML/CSS for online role-play forums, where visuals were just as expressive as words. We loved to write stories and showcase them in beautiful layouts and environments. That playful curiosity eventually evolved into a passion for creating real-world solutions through code

While I have a background in Electronics Engineering, most of my technical growth has come from hands-on projects, self-driven learning, and the thrill of problem-solving. I enjoy figuring things out—whether that means debugging code, structuring a backend system, or just understanding how people think. Psychology and math have always fascinated me for the same reason: they’re puzzles waiting to be solved.

I’m still exploring where I fit best in tech, but I’m open, flexible, and eager to learn. I especially love projects that solve practical problems—like my thesis project, which was born from my own frustration of losing my car in parking lots.

Outside of programming, I enjoy gaming, reading, writing, crochet, and learning music (currently tackling the recorder!). I’ve lived between Spain and Romania, moved often, and adapted to new environments all my life—something that’s shaped my ability to stay focused and level-headed under pressure, even when things around me feel chaotic.

Above all, I value comfort, patience, and mutual support in a work environment. I believe the best teams are the ones where you can be yourself while growing alongside others.
